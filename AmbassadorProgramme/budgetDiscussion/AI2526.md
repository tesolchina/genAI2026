# Hong Kong Government Budget & Plan on AI (2025–26)

This document summarises the government’s Budget 2025–26 and related policy on artificial intelligence (AI), drawn from the Budget Speech, official news, and LegCo Q&As.

---

## 1. Budget 2025–26: Main AI-related measures

### 1.1 Hong Kong AI Research and Development Institute (AIRDI)

- **Funding:** HK$1 billion set aside in the 2025–26 Budget.
- **Purpose:** Establish the **Hong Kong AI Research and Development Institute** to promote the application of research outcomes.
- **Lead:** Digital Policy Office is responsible for the institute’s formulation.
- **Role:** Spearhead both R&D and industrial applications of AI; develop AI as a “core industry” and empower traditional industries to upgrade through AI adoption.
- **LegCo approval:** Funding was approved; the institute is to be established in **2026**.
- **Planned functions (from LegCo):** Facilitate upstream AI R&D, midstream and downstream transformation of R&D outcomes, and expansion of application scenarios; provide professional advice to industries and enterprises on AI application difficulties.

**Sources:**

- [The 2025-26 Budget – Upgrading Industries](https://www.budget.gov.hk/2025/eng/ui.html) — “Set aside $1 billion to establish the Hong Kong Artificial Intelligence Research and Development Institute to promote the application of research outcomes”
- [$1b set aside for AI R&D institute](https://www.news.gov.hk/eng/2025/02/20250226/20250226_093511_297.html) (news.gov.hk, 26 Feb 2025)
- [LCQ13: Promoting development of artificial intelligence](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202510/15/P2025101500264.htm) (15 Oct 2025)
- [LCQ2: Alignment with country's efforts in promoting artificial intelligence development](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202509/25/P2025092500554.htm) (25 Sep 2025)

### 1.2 Computing and infrastructure

- **Cyberport AI Supercomputing Centre (AISC):** Computing power on schedule to reach **3,000 petaFLOPS** in 2025.
- **AI Subsidy Scheme:** HK$3 billion to support large-scale AI scientific research and applications; over ten major projects approved to date.
- **Sandy Ridge (North District):** A site put up for tender for a **data facility cluster** to provide advanced computing power and support data and AI-related industries.

### 1.3 Other I&T measures in the same Budget

- **I&T Industry-Oriented Fund:** HK$10 billion to channel market capital into emerging and future industries of strategic importance (including AI); LegCo funding approval targeted for mid-2025.
- **Pilot Manufacturing & Production Line Upgrade Support Scheme:** HK$100 million over two years; up to HK$250,000 per enterprise on a 1:2 matching basis for advanced production technologies.
- **I&T Accelerator Pilot Scheme:** HK$180 million; up to HK$30 million per professional start-up service agency on a 1:2 matching basis.
- **Third InnoHK research cluster:** Invitation for proposals focused on advanced manufacturing, materials, energy and sustainable development.
- **HKIC events:** First International Conference on Embodied AI Robot and International Young Scientist Forum on Artificial Intelligence (hosted by Hong Kong Investment Corporation).
- **HKEX “technology enterprises channel” (TECH):** To help specialist technology and biotechnology companies (including Mainland-listed) prepare for listing and raise funds.

## 2. Strategic framework (beyond the Budget figure)

- **Hong Kong Innovation and Technology Development Blueprint:** Strategic focus on AI with emphasis on “strengthening infrastructure and promoting the application-oriented approach.”
- **2025 Policy Address:** Broaden AI applications to empower industries; consolidate strengths in AI research, talent, funding, and data.
- **Alignment with national “AI Plus” initiative:** The State Council’s “Opinions on Deepening the Implementation of the 'AI Plus' Initiative” (Aug 2025) sets phased objectives (e.g. deep integration with six key areas by 2027; full AI empowerment for high-quality development by 2030). Hong Kong’s multi-dimensional AI policies are stated to be in line with this and with global AI development.

---

## 3. Research, talent and data (from LegCo replies)

- **AIR@InnoHK:** 16 R&D laboratories in AI and robotics; over a thousand experts.
- **Frontier Technology Research Support Scheme:** HK$3 billion to support UGC-funded universities to attract top international researchers in AI and other frontier technologies (launched Sept 2025).
- **Talent and education:** HK$2 billion from the Quality Education Fund for digital education; Blueprint for Digital Education including an **“AI literacy” learning framework**; pilot schemes on AI education and teacher training; digital platforms and tools with AI under the e-Learning Ancillary Facilities Programme. VTC’s Hong Kong Institute of Information Technology offers AI-related programmes (e.g. Higher Diploma in AI and Smart Technology). UGC encourages more STEAM and AI-related programmes.
- **Data:** Open Data Portal with 5,600+ datasets; GBA “Standard Contract” facilitation measure for cross-boundary data flow; exploration of compliant flow of Mainland data to the Hong Kong Park of the Hetao Co-operation Zone for research and AI application testing.
- **SMEs:** Digital Transformation Support Pilot Programme enhanced to help SMEs apply AI solutions with matching resources; AIRDI to provide professional advice to industries on AI application.

---

## 4. Governance and guidelines

- **Ethical AI Framework,** **Principles of Data Governance,** and **Hong Kong Generative AI Technical and Application Guideline** (Digital Policy Office).
- **Privacy:** PCPD guidelines on AI governance and personal data.
- **Legislation:** Department of Justice to establish an inter-departmental working group to review legislation to support wider application of AI.
