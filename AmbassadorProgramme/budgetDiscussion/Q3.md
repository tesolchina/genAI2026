# Q3: From an academic perspective — biggest strengths and shortcomings of Hong Kong’s AI policy

## Question

**Chinese:** 从学术角度看，目前香港AI政策的最大优势与短板分别是什么？

**English:** From an academic perspective, what are the biggest strengths and shortcomings of Hong Kong's current AI policies?

---

## Simon’s initial comments

- **AI is not one set of technologies**; it’s very open-ended. It has the **potential to transform the entire economy** and is developing very fast.
- **Strengths:** Our **talents and financial markets** are our strength; if the government can leverage that, it would be great. (Reference: [Hong Kong picks 3 departments to trial use of AI](https://www.scmp.com/news/hong-kong/society/article/3327092/hong-kong-picks-3-departments-trial-use-ai-raise-government-efficiency).)
- **Shortcomings:** **Most people do not have the competence to embrace AI**, yet these people are **making decisions that can hold society back**. We need to **encourage young people to embrace AI** and **fundamentally redesign the entire economy**, including the **education sector**.

## Draft answer (for interview preparation)

From an academic perspective, I’d frame it like this.

**Strengths.** Hong Kong’s **talents and financial markets** are real advantages. We have strong research capacity (e.g. InnoHK, AIR@InnoHK labs), a deep capital market that can fund and list AI companies (e.g. Zhipu IPO, the “AI-for-finance” ecosystem), and a role as an **East–West bridge** where different AI models and capital can meet. The government is starting to **leverage** that—e.g. the three-department AI trial (Companies Registry, Transport Department, Buildings Department), the plan for AI in 200 public service procedures by end-2027, and the broader push around AIRDI and the I&T Fund. So on paper we have a clear **strategic direction**: strengthen infrastructure and promote application-oriented AI, in line with the national “AI Plus” initiative. If the government keeps leveraging our **talent and financial hub** position, that’s a solid base.

**Shortcomings.** AI is **not one technology** but a fast-moving, open-ended set of capabilities that can **transform the whole economy**. The main risk isn’t only budget or infrastructure; it’s **people and institutions**. **Most people—including many in positions of authority—still don’t have the competence to embrace AI.** When those same people are **making decisions** (in government, schools, universities, firms), they can **hold society back** without meaning to: slow adoption, poor procurement, curricula that don’t prepare young people. So we need to **encourage young people to embrace AI** and **redesign the economy and education** so that literacy and competence catch up with the technology. Research also points to a **usage–trust gap** in Hong Kong (e.g. many users not evaluating AI outputs, mixed trust in AI systems), which underlines that guidelines and infrastructure alone aren’t enough without **literacy and governance**. Another issue is **fragmented oversight**—no single body for AI—and the fact that key documents are **non-binding**, which can create uncertainty.

In short: **strengths** = talent, financial markets, and the government’s willingness to leverage them with trials and strategy; **shortcomings** = widespread **lack of competence and literacy** among decision-makers and the public, and the need to **redesign education and the economy** so that adoption and governance keep pace with the speed of AI.

## 參考答案（中文，含超連結）

從學術角度，我會這樣概括。**優勢：** 香港的**人才與金融市場**是實實在在的優勢：我們有較強的科研能力（如 [InnoHK、AIR@InnoHK 實驗室](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202510/15/P2025101500264.htm)）、能為 AI 企業融資與上市的資本市場（如智譜 IPO、[「AI 金融」生態](https://www.fsdc.org.hk/en/media/hong-kong-emerges-as-a-global-ai-for-finance-hub-where-capital-markets-regulation-and-production-scale-deployment-converge)）、以及作為**東西橋樑**讓不同 AI 模型與資本匯聚的角色。政府已開始**槓桿**這些優勢——例如[三部門 AI 試行](https://www.scmp.com/news/hong-kong/society/article/3327092/hong-kong-picks-3-departments-trial-use-ai-raise-government-efficiency)（公司註冊處、運輸署、屋宇署）、[2027 年底前 200 項公共程序引入 AI](https://www.scmp.com/news/hong-kong/society/article/3325910/hong-kong-roll-out-ai-use-200-public-service-procedures-end-2027)、以及 AIRDI 與 I&T 基金等；[官方答問](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202510/15/P2025101500264.htm)也把研究、基建、數據、產業應用與人才列為多維度政策，並對接國家「AI+」。**短板：** AI **不是單一技術**，而是可**改造整個經濟**、發展極快的一組開放能力。主要風險不只在預算或基建，而在**人與制度**——**多數人（包括不少掌權者）仍缺乏擁抱 AI 的能力**，而正是這些人在**做決定**（政府、學校、大學、企業），可能無意中**拖慢社會**：採用慢、採購差、課程未為年輕人做好準備。因此我們要**鼓勵年輕人擁抱 AI** 並**從根本重設計經濟與教育**，讓素養與能力追上技術。[研究也指出本港存在使用與信任的落差](https://digital.nemko.com/insights/hong-kong-ai-governance-in-2025)（例如不少人未評估 AI 輸出、對 AI 信任參差），說明單靠指引與基建不足，還需**素養與治理**；[評論同樣強調 AI 素養對香港未來競爭力的關鍵](https://www.scmp.com/special-reports/article/3322237/ai-literacy-key-hong-kongs-future-supremacy-global-education-race)。另一問題是**監管分散**、缺乏單一 AI 監督機構，且關鍵文件**不具法律約束力**，會帶來不確定性。簡言之：**優勢** = 人才、金融市場與政府以試行與戰略槓桿它們的意願；**短板** = 決策者與公眾**普遍缺乏能力與素養**，以及必須**重設計教育與經濟**，讓採用與治理跟上 AI 的速度。
