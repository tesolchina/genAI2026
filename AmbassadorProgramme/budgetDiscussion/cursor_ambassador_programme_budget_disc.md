- **Question:** Where should the government prioritise: computing power, talent, industrial application, or regulation?
- **Simon’s points reflected:** Education hub + GBA collaboration (no strong manufacturing base); AI applications that create value; businesses more proactive than government/universities; need for policies and support.
- **Links:** Budget UI, LCQ13, Young Reporter, HKSTP–GBA AI+ seminar, FLAIR/HKPC, InnoHK, LCQ5 (enterprises & AI), SME adoption articles, Digital Policy Office industry development, Nemko AI governance 2025, AI2526.md.
- **Draft answer:** Doesn’t pick one bucket; stresses (1) industrial application + GBA collaboration and (2) closing the motivation gap (business vs government/universities) with policies and support; regulation as important but not the top priority next.

### **Q3.md** — *AmbassadorProgramme/budgetDiscussion/Q3.md*
- **Question:** From an academic perspective, what are the biggest strengths and shortcomings of Hong Kong’s current AI policies?
- **Simon’s points reflected:** AI as open-ended and economy-transforming; strengths = talents and financial markets; shortcomings = many people (including decision-makers) lack competence and can hold society back; need to encourage youth and redesign economy and education.
- **Links:** SCMP “3 departments” trial, SCMP “200 procedures by 2027”, SCMP on financial centre edge, FSDC AI-for-finance hub pieces, LCQ13, Nemko governance (fragmentation, usage–trust gap), SCMP AI literacy article, AI2526.md.
- **Draft answer:** Strengths = talent, financial markets, hub role, and government starting to leverage them (trials, 200 procedures, AIRDI). Shortcomings = widespread lack of competence/literacy among decision-makers and public; need to redesign education and economy; fragmented oversight and non-binding guidelines.
