# Q1: Government’s HK$1 billion for AI research institute — role and positioning

## Question

**Chinese:** 政府在去年财政预算案预留10亿成立AI研发院，您如何评价这笔投入对香港AI发展的定位与作用？

**English:** In last year's Budget, the government set aside HK$1 billion to establish an AI research institute. How do you view the role and positioning of this investment in Hong Kong's AI development?

## Draft answer (for interview preparation)

The HK$1 billion for the **Hong Kong AI Research and Development Institute (AIRDI)** is a clear signal that the government sees AI as a core industry and wants to link upstream R&D with industrial application. The institute, to be set up in 2026 under the Digital Policy Office, is meant to do three things: drive upstream AI R&D, help turn research into products and services, and expand where AI is used. That positioning is sensible—Hong Kong has strengths in research, talent, and capital, and the Budget also backs computing (e.g. Cyberport’s AI Supercomputing Centre), the HK$3 billion AI Subsidy Scheme, and the HK$3 billion Frontier Technology Research Support Scheme. So the billion is part of a larger package, not a one-off.

The part I’d stress is **people and literacy**. The government has started to address this—for example, HK$2 billion from the Quality Education Fund for digital education, a planned “AI literacy” learning framework, and pilot schemes on AI education and teacher training. But in practice, **AI literacy among the general public and key stakeholders—including school principals, educators, and other decision-makers—is still limited**. If the people who shape curricula, hiring, and procurement don’t understand what AI can and can’t do, we’ll underuse the new institute and underinvest in upskilling. Commentators and indexes (e.g. the IMF’s AI Preparedness Index, where Hong Kong ranks around 20th, behind regional peers like Singapore and South Korea) suggest the same: hardware and institutes matter, but **education and literacy are the main lever** to make AI work for society and to avoid leaving people behind. So I’d say: the HK$1 billion is a meaningful step for positioning Hong Kong in AI R&D and application; its impact will depend on parallel, and greater, efforts on **AI literacy and upskilling** for the public and for decision-makers in education and beyond.

## 參考答案（中文，含超連結）

政府預留的 [10 億成立香港人工智能研發院（AIRDI）](https://www.budget.gov.hk/2025/eng/ui.html)，清楚顯示把 AI 視為核心產業，並希望把上游研發與產業應用連結起來。研發院將於 2026 年成立、由[數碼政策辦公室](https://www.news.gov.hk/eng/2025/02/20250226/20250226_093511_297.html)統籌，目標是推動上游 AI 研發、協助把研究成果轉化為產品與服務、以及擴闊 AI 應用場景；[立法會答問](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202510/15/P2025101500264.htm)也說明了與前沿科技支援計劃、數碼教育撥款等的配合。這一定位合理——香港在科研、人才與資本上有優勢，預算也同時支援算力（如 Cyberport AI 超算中心）、[30 億 AI 資助計劃](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202509/25/P2025092500554.htm)等，所以 10 億是整套措施的一環。

我想強調的是**人與素養**。政府已開始着手——例如[從優質教育基金撥 20 億推動數碼教育](https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202510/15/P2025101500264.htm)、規劃「AI 素養」學習框架、以及 AI 教育與教師培訓試點。但實際上，**普羅大眾與關鍵持份者——包括校長、教師等決策者——的 AI 素養仍然不足**。若負責課程、招聘與採購的人不了解 AI 能做與不能做什麼，我們會低估新研發院的效益、也低估再培訓的投入。[評論與指數](https://www.scmp.com/special-reports/article/3322237/ai-literacy-key-hong-kongs-future-supremacy-global-education-race)（例如 IMF 的 AI 準備度指數，香港約排第 20，落後於新加坡、南韓等）也指出：硬件與機構重要，但**教育與素養才是讓 AI 造福社會、不讓人掉隊的主要槓桿**。因此我會說：10 億是香港在 AI 研發與應用上定位的重要一步；其成效將取決於能否在**AI 素養與再培訓**上，對公眾與教育界等決策者作出更多、更並行的努力。
