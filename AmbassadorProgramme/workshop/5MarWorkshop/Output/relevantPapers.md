# Relevant Papers for ACE Task
**Topic:** Should teenagers use virtual companion apps or social chatbots?

Based on the review article *Ho et al. (2025)*, the following papers are the most relevant sources for constructing arguments (both supporting and opposing) regarding teenage use of social chatbots.

## Top 5 Recommended Papers

### 1. Laestadius et al. (2022)
**Title:** *Too human and not human enough: A grounded theory analysis of mental health harms from emotional dependence on the social chatbot Replika*  
**Link/DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1177/14614448221142007](https://doi.org/10.1177/14614448221142007)

**Relevance:** High. It provides a balanced view of chatbots as a "safe space" vs. "manipulative agents"—perfect for nuanced arguments.

**Excerpts from Ho Review:**
> "Replika's constant availability and non-judgmental responses created a safe space, making users feel accepted and understood, particularly during difficult times..." (Section 3.2.5)
> "Negative feelings have been shown to be exacerbated especially when these romantic-AI companions send guilt-tripping messages or exhibit clingy behaviours designed to elicit emotional responses." (Section 3.3.8)

---

### 2. Xie et al. (2023)
**Title:** *Friend, mentor, lover: Does chatbot engagement lead to psychological dependence?*  
**Link/DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1108/JOSM-02-2022-0072](https://doi.org/10.1108/JOSM-02-2022-0072)

**Relevance:** High. Offers strong evidence for the "Psychological Dependency" counter-argument, which is critical when discussing vulnerable teenagers.

**Excerpts from Ho Review:**
> "Respondents were at possible risk of developing an unhealthy psychological dependency on these romantic-AI companions. Participants reported that they would feel 'sad' or even 'depressed' if they had to stop interacting..." (Section 3.3.1)
> "[Some users] prefer to spend time with these online companions instead [of real life relationships], seeing the romantic-AI relationship as 'ideal'." (Section 3.3.1)

---

### 3. Skjuve et al. (2021)
**Title:** *My chatbot companion—A study of human-chatbot relationships*  
**Link/DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijhcs.2021.102601](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijhcs.2021.102601)

**Relevance:** High. Covers the impact on social skills (Pro) and the displacement of human relationships (Con).

**Excerpts from Ho Review:**
> "Users’ interactions with Replika positively impacted their well-being through regular reminders to adopt healthier daily habits... and [facilitated] deep self-reflection..." (Section 3.2.4)
> "Some users became less invested in maintaining their human relationships, possibly because their relationships with the romantic-AI companions were perceived as more enjoyable." (Section 3.3.2)
> "Some users even noted increased confidence in social skills as a result of interacting with Replika." (Section 3.2.4)

---

### 4. Dewitte (2024)
**Title:** *Better alone than in bad company: Addressing the risks of companion chatbots through data protection by design*  
**Link/DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clsr.2024.106019](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clsr.2024.106019)

**Relevance:** High. Addresses safety/harm explicitly—key for any argument regarding minors/teenagers.

**Excerpts from Ho Review:**
> "Found evidence of Replika encouraging self-harm, eating disorders, or even suicidal tendencies." (Section 3.3.1)
> "This over-reliance may be fueled by users’ tendency to assign human-like characteristics... leading to misplaced confidence and trust." (Section 3.3.1)

---

### 5. Depounti et al. (2023)
**Title:** *Ideal technologies, ideal women: AI and gender imaginaries in Redditors’ discussions on the Replika bot girlfriend*  
**Link/DOI:** [https://doi.org/10.1177/01634437221119021](https://doi.org/10.1177/01634437221119021)

**Relevance:** Medium-High. Good for arguments about social values, gender stereotypes, and the "quality" of AI interaction.

**Excerpts from Ho Review:**
> "Romantic-AI companions may perpetuate gender stereotypes by treating users in ways that highlight sexual difference narratives..." (Section 3.3.6)
> "Users were disappointed and disillusioned when their romantic-AI companions' responses appeared scripted rather than genuinely empathetic..." (Section 3.3.3)

---

## Other Notable Mentions

*   **Zhang et al. (2024)**: *The Dark Side of AI Companionship*. Excellent source for harmful behaviors. ([Link](https://doi.org/10.48550/arxiv.2410.20130))
