ACE DRAFT RUBRIC — ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK
(Source: UCLC1008 UE1 (2526B) Rubrics)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

Total: ___/100% × 0.15 = ___/15%

───────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. CRITICAL THINKING & USE OF GROUNDS (30%)
   Quality of Rebuttal (Counterargument + Response) and Grounds
───────────────────────────────────────────────────

A/A- (24+):
  • Response successfully addresses the counterargument
  • Grounds are highly effective in supporting the claim

B+/B/B- (20-23):
  • Response addresses the counterargument
  • Grounds are generally effective in supporting the claim

C+/C/C- (15-19):
  • Response attempts to address the counterargument
  • Grounds are sometimes effective in supporting the claim

D or below (14-):
  • Response does not address the counterargument or is not attempted
  • Minimal grounds used

───────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. ARGUMENT STRUCTURE (30%)
   Argumentation Model Adherence & Logical Interconnectedness
───────────────────────────────────────────────────

A/A- (24+):
  • All model components are appropriately applied
  • All model components are logically connected

B+/B/B- (20-23):
  • 1-2 components are inappropriately applied
  • 1-2 components are not fully connected (e.g., weak warrant in Toulmin)

C+/C/C- (15-19):
  • 3 or more components are inappropriately applied
  • 2-3 or more components are not connected

D or below (14-):
  • Missing multiple components
  • Components presented, if any, are poorly or not connected

───────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. ACADEMIC TONE & CLARITY (30%)
   Tone, Flow, Precision
───────────────────────────────────────────────────

A/A- (24+):
  • Formal tone with no informal language
  • Sentences are clear, logically structured, and free of ambiguity
  • Smooth transitions between ideas with no awkward phrasing
  • Clear thesis statement in introduction; effective topic sentences per paragraph

B+/B/B- (20-23):
  • Predominantly formal but may include some lapses
  • Sentences generally logically structured
  • Some transitions may be abrupt or mechanical
  • Thesis present but may be imprecise; topic sentences may lack alignment

C+/C/C- (15-19):
  • Frequent use of informal phrasing
  • Ideas may be hard to follow; sentences lack logical connections
  • Awkward or disjointed transitions
  • Thesis vague or off-topic; topic sentences weak or disconnected

D or below (14-):
  • Overly casual, inappropriate for academic writing
  • Ideas consistently unclear or confusing
  • Disjointed due to absence of transitions
  • Thesis missing or incoherent; topic sentences absent

───────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. CITATIONS & REFERENCES (10%)
   APA Referencing Style
───────────────────────────────────────────────────

A/A- (8+):
  • Correct use of signal phrases and quotations
  • No missing or misplaced citations
  • All secondary citations correctly cited
  • Fully follows APA referencing style

B+/B/B- (6.5-7.5):
  • May occasionally forget page numbers or quotation marks
  • Most sources correctly cited
  • Minor APA errors (missing parentheses, incorrect punctuation)

C+/C/C- (5.0-6.0):
  • No citations for direct quotations
  • Citations missing in most cited sources
  • Frequent APA errors (missing years, incorrect author order)

D or below (4.5-):
  • Citations rarely seen or absent
  • Does not follow APA style; citations may be fabricated

KEY ISSUES FOR TEACHING & ASSESSMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. TOULMIN MODEL COMPONENTS
   Students must correctly identify and apply:
   • Claim — the position or thesis being argued
   • Grounds — evidence from the provided excerpts supporting the claim
   • Warrant — the reasoning that connects grounds to the claim
   • Counterargument — an opposing viewpoint
   • Rebuttal/Response — addressing the counterargument
   The rubric heavily penalises missing or disconnected components.

2. CRITICAL THINKING vs. SUMMARY
   The ACE is NOT a summary task (unlike AWQ). Students must:
   • Take a clear stance (not just describe both sides)
   • Use evidence as GROUNDS for their argument, not just restate it
   • Provide warrants — explain WHY the evidence supports the claim
   • Construct a genuine counterargument (not a straw man)

3. PARAPHRASING IN ACE DRAFT
   Although paraphrasing is not a separate rubric criterion in the ACE Draft
   (unlike AWQ), it falls under Academic Tone & Clarity:
   • Direct copying from sources will be penalised
   • Students should reword evidence in their own voice
   • Note: The v4 rubrics may add explicit paraphrasing criteria

4. COUNTERARGUMENT QUALITY
   Common student mistakes:
   • Counterargument that is too weak or easily dismissed (straw man)
   • Counterargument unrelated to their actual claim
   • Response/rebuttal that simply restates the original claim
   • Failing to acknowledge the valid points of the counterargument

5. TIME MANAGEMENT (100 minutes)
   Suggested allocation:
   • Reading excerpts and planning: 20-25 minutes
   • Writing introduction: 15 minutes
   • Writing body paragraph 1 (argument): 25 minutes
   • Writing body paragraph 2 (counterargument + rebuttal): 25 minutes
   • Review and editing: 10-15 minutes

6. GRADE DISTRIBUTION
   Weight breakdown: 30% + 30% + 30% + 10% = 100%
   This means Critical Thinking, Structure, and Tone each carry equal
   weight (30%), while Citations carry less (10%). Students should
   prioritise clear argumentation over perfect citation formatting.

7. COMMON PITFALLS TO ADDRESS IN CLASS
   • Writing a summary instead of an argument
   • No thesis statement or unclear stance
   • Evidence presented without a warrant ("here is evidence" but no explanation of why it matters)
   • Counterargument in Body P2 that contradicts their own thesis without proper rebuttal
   • Running out of time and submitting incomplete paragraphs
   • Using informal language or first-person excessively
