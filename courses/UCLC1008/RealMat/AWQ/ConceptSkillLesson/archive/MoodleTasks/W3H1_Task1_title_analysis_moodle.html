Week 3 Hour 1 — Task 1: Title analysis (subject, context, stance)

Module 1 · Activity 2.1 · Rubric: Summary Accuracy (coverage)

Reading materials: UCLC1008 (2526a) Sample AWQ — Articles A & B (lessonsMoodle/Mat).

1. Reading materials

Use the two article titles below. For each title, identify Subject matter, Context, and Purpose/stance (Support / Promote / Reject / Doubt / Discuss).

Article A

Supporting schools to use face recognition systems: a continuance intention perspective of elementary school parents in China
Hong et al. (2022)

Article B

Facial recognition technology in schools: critical questions and concerns
Andrejevic & Selwyn (2020)

Framework

  • Subject matter: broad topic (e.g. face recognition in schools)
  • Context: specific focus (e.g. parents in China; continuance intention; schools)
  • Purpose/stance: Support / Promote / Reject / Doubt / Discuss — based on word choice (e.g. “supporting”, “critical questions”, “concerns”)

2. What to do

Write one sentence that states Article B’s subject + context + stance. Use the title only (no need to read the full article).

3. How to reply (Moodle forum)

Your post

  1. Click Reply in this forum discussion.
  2. Write one sentence that states Article B’s subject + context + stance.
  3. Post your reply.

Example format: “Article B focuses on [subject] in [context]; the title suggests a [stance] stance (e.g. doubt/critical) because of phrases like ‘critical questions’ and ‘concerns’.”

Reply to one peer

  1. Read a classmate’s post.
  2. Click Reply to their post.
  3. Write: Does their sentence match the title? Answer Yes or No, and give one brief reason.

Example: “Yes. They correctly identified the subject (FRT in schools), context (schools), and stance (doubt/critical) from ‘critical questions and concerns’.”

4. Teacher: verbal prompts

Multiple-choice (optional, ~1 min)

What is the stance of Article B’s title?

  • A) Support
  • B) Promote
  • C) Doubt / Critical
  • D) Reject

Pair discussion (~1 min)

Ask students to work in pairs and say aloud:

  • “What are the subject, context, and stance for Article A?”
  • “What are the subject, context, and stance for Article B?”

Tip: Use these prompts before or after students post on the forum, as fits your class flow.
