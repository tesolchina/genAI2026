Week 3 Hour 3 — Task 9: No personal bias (report sources only)

Module 2 · Rubric: Summary Accuracy (free from personal bias; no “I think”)

Reading materials: Rule, examples, and sentence to rewrite (below) — Sample AWQ Sample Scripts, lessonsMoodle/Mat.

1. Reading materials

Rule for AWQ summaries

Report only what the sources say. Do not add your own views, speculation, or phrases like “I think,” “perhaps,” “maybe,” “in my view,” “we face,” or “I believe.”

Example — Unacceptable (Student B style)

“Article B says facial recognition is bad because there is no option for students to self-curate and restrict what data they share. Students don't have freedom to choose to opt out. Maybe students don't care, or it is not a big problem. The technology might just help schools be more organized (Andrejevic & Selwyn). So, I think that Article A says parents like facial recognition because of its value…”

Example — Acceptable

“Hong et al. (2022) found that parents tended to support FRT despite privacy concerns. Andrejevic and Selwyn (2020) argue that FRT creates inescapable surveillance and undermines consent.”

Use author + year + reporting verb + source’s claim only. No personal view.

2. What to do

Rewrite the sentence below so it has no personal view. Use the source (Andrejevic & Selwyn, 2020) and report only their claims. Post your rewrite.

Sentence to rewrite: “I believe that Andrejevic and Selwyn (2020) raise important concerns about consent.”

3. How to reply (Moodle forum)

Your post

  1. Click Reply in this forum discussion.
  2. Write your rewrite (no “I believe,” “I think,” or similar; attribute the claim to the authors).
  3. Post your reply.

Example: “Andrejevic and Selwyn (2020) raise important concerns about consent regarding FRT in schools.”

Reply to one peer

  1. Read a classmate’s post.
  2. Click Reply to their post.
  3. Write: Is their version free of personal bias? Answer Yes or No, and give one brief reason.

Example: “Yes. They removed ‘I believe’ and reported only what the authors argue.”

4. Teacher: verbal prompts

Multiple-choice (optional, ~1 min)

Which is acceptable in an AWQ summary?

  • A) I think both articles show that FRT is controversial.
  • B) Perhaps schools will adopt FRT anyway.
  • C) Hong et al. (2022) found that parents tended to support FRT despite privacy concerns.
  • D) In my view, Article B is more convincing.

Pair discussion (~1 min)

Ask students to work in pairs and say aloud:

  • “Which option would you choose, and why?”
  • “Say an unacceptable version of C that adds personal bias.”

Tip: Use these prompts before or after students post on the forum, as fits your class flow.
