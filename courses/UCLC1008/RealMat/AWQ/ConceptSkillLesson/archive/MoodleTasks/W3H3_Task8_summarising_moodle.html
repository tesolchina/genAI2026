Week 3 Hour 3 — Task 8: Summarising (key points, own words)

Module 2 · Part 2 · Rubric: Summary Accuracy (coverage); Paraphrasing (own words)

Reading materials: Module 2 — McClellan (2020) (Examples of summarising). Not from the Sample AWQ.

1. Reading materials

Steps to summarising

  1. Read thoroughly — understand content and structure.
  2. Identify key points — main ideas, arguments; distinguish from supporting details.
  3. Eliminate extraneous information — omit raw stats, long examples, detailed methodology unless briefly summarised.
  4. Use your own words — no copying.
  5. Keep the author’s tone — e.g. neutral, critical.

Source text — McClellan (2020)

McClellan (2020) — on facial recognition technology, regulation, and public concerns. Module 2, Examples of summarising.

The use of facial recognition technology (FRT) has continued to grow and expand, providing helpful and creative uses in almost all aspects of life. The expansive use of the technology in social media, in particular, has begun to facilitate conversation amongst not only lawmakers, but everyday citizens as well. Although fairly new and exciting to some, facial recognition technology has become increasingly worrisome for many, in part due to the lack of regulations surrounding the technology. As facial recognition technology evolves and expands, there is an increasing need for regulation at the federal level. These regulations should consider and incorporate language that will allow the technology to continue providing benefits to society, but also ensure that limitations and penalties are placed on users of the technology to protect citizens.

(McClellan, 2020, p. 364)

Key points to include: benefits of FRT (growth, uses, e.g. in social media); worries (lack of regulation); need for federal regulation; regulations should balance benefits and protections (limitations, penalties). Omit or shorten: lengthy examples, repeated phrases.

Condense to 1–2 sentences: keep the main claims (benefits, worries, call for regulation), drop the elaboration.

2. What to do

Summarise the McClellan (2020) paragraph in 1–2 sentences, in your own words, with no copying. Keep the main claims; drop the elaboration.

3. How to reply (Moodle forum)

Your post

  1. Click Reply in this forum discussion.
  2. Write your summary (1–2 sentences, own words, no copying; main claims only, no elaboration).
  3. Add an in-text citation (McClellan, 2020) when you refer to the author’s claims.
  4. Post your reply.

Example: McClellan (2020) argues that although FRT has grown and offers benefits in many areas, including social media and public debate, lack of regulation has increased public concern. The author calls for federal regulation that both preserves the technology’s benefits and introduces limits and penalties to protect citizens.

Reply to one peer

  1. Read a classmate’s post.
  2. Click Reply to their post.
  3. Write: Are key points included and extraneous detail omitted? Answer Yes or No, and give one brief reason.

Example: “Yes. They covered benefits, concerns about regulation, and the call for federal rules that balance benefits and protections, without copying long examples.”

4. Teacher: verbal prompts

Multiple-choice (optional, ~1 min)

When summarising McClellan’s (2020) paragraph, which should you emphasise?

  • A) Every example (e.g. social media, lawmakers) in full detail.
  • B) Main points: benefits of FRT, worries about regulation, need for federal regulation, and balance of benefits and protections.
  • C) Only the first sentence.
  • D) Your personal view on FRT regulation.

Pair discussion (~1 min)

Ask students to work in pairs and say aloud:

  • “What are 2–3 key points from McClellan’s (2020) paragraph?”
  • “What would you omit when summarising it?”

Tip: Use these prompts before or after students post on the forum, as fits your class flow.
