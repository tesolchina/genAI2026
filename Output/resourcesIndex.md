# ACE Test Preparation Resources Index

This index summarizes the project files and provides a guide on how to use them to prepare for the upcoming ACE Argument Construction and Evaluation test.

## Overview
Based on the review of `Data/module 3` and `Literature`, the upcoming test involves writing a short argumentative essay (approx. 400 words) in 100 minutes. The tentative topic is strict: **"Should teenagers use virtual companion apps or social chatbots?"**.

## File Summaries and Usage Guide

### 1. Core Instructions & Rules
*   **File**: `Data/module 3/draftInstructions.md`
*   **Summary**: Outlines the test format: 100 minutes, handwritten, no AI/tools. It specifies the structure: Introduction + Body Paragraph 1 (Argument) + Body Paragraph 2 (Counterargument & Rebuttal).
*   **How to use**: 
    1.  **Memorize the structure**: You only have 100 minutes. Know exactly what goes in each of the 3 paragraphs before you walk in.
    2.  **Note the constraints**: 400 words is short. Do not over-write. Focus on quality of argument over quantity.

### 2. Assessment Criteria (The "Rules of the Game")
*   **File**: `Data/module 3/rubrics.md`
*   **Summary**: Detailed grading criteria. 
    *   **30% Critical Thinking**: Quality of Rebuttal (Counterargument + Response) and Grounds (Evidence).
    *   **30% Argument Structure**: adherence to the specific Argumentation Model (likely Toulmin).
*   **How to use**:
    1.  **Self-Correction**: After writing a practice paragraph, check it against the "A/A-" column. Did you include a *refutation* in your rebuttal? Is your evidence *specific*?
    2.  **Focus Area**: The "Rebuttal" is a specific skills category. Spend extra time ensuring you know how to "turn" a counterargument back to your side.

### 3. Source Material (The "Content")
*   **File**: `Data/Literature/HoReview.md`
*   **Summary**: A review article titled *Computers in Human Behavior Reports*. It discusses social chatbots, potentially covering their benefits (companionship) and drawbacks (replacement of human interaction).
*   **How to use**:
    1.  **Mine for Evidence**: This is likely the "Source Article" mentioned in the instructions. Read it and extract 3-4 key quotes/statistics for *each side* of the argument (Pro-Chatbot and Anti-Chatbot).
    2.  **Pre-plan Arguments**: Since you know the topic might be about "social chatbots", draft your "Grounds" (evidence) based on this file *now*.

### 4. Practice Materials (The "Gym")
*   **File**: `Data/module 3/practiceWritingTask.md`
*   **Summary**: A full practice test simulation. Current topic is "Working from home", but the *structure* is identical to the real test.
*   **How to use**:
    1.  **Timed Run**: Set a timer for 100 minutes. Write the essay on the WFH topic using the provided articles in the file.
    2.  **Structure Drill**: Use this to practice the "Intro -> Claim -> Warrant -> Counter -> Rebuttal" flow without worrying about the new topic yet.

### 5. Strategy & Skill Building
*   **Files**: 
    *   `Data/module 3/argumentModels.md` (Explains the Toulmin model: Claim, Grounds, Warrant, Backing, Qualifier, Rebuttal).
    *   `Data/module 3/respondCounterargument.md` (Drills on how to respond to counterarguments).
    *   `Data/module 3/strategiesCounterargument.md` (Specific strategies for the counterargument paragraph).
    *   `Data/module 3/AIprompts.md` (Prompts to use with AI *before* the test to analyze arguments and check your practice work. **Do not use during the test.**).
*   **How to use**:
    1.  **Terminology**: Ensure you understand what a "Warrant" is. If your essay lacks the "explanation of why the evidence supports the claim," you lose points.
    2.  **Refining the Rebuttal**: Use `respondCounterargument.md` to practice the specific transition phrases ("While it is true that...", "However, this overlooks...").

### 6. Samples
*   **Files**: `Data/module 3/ACE sample 1.md`, `ACE sample 2.md`
*   **Note**: *Currently these files appear to be empty or contain only placeholders. Use with caution or inform the instructor.*

## Recommended Study Plan (Next 2 Weeks)

1.  **Day 1-3**: Read `HoReview.md` deeply. Create a "Cheat Sheet" of 5 strong arguments for Chatbots and 5 against, with page number citations.
2.  **Day 4-7**: Study `argumentModels.md`. Write *just* the Body Paragraph 1 for the Chatbot topic, ensuring you have Claim + Evidence + Warrant.
3.  **Day 8-10**: Focus entirely on the Rebuttal (Body Paragraph 2). Use `strategiesCounterargument.md` to write 3 different rebuttals to common anti-chatbot arguments.
4.  **Day 11-13**: Do a full timed practice run using `practiceWritingTask.md` (even if you've done it before, do it for speed).
5.  **Day 14**: Review `draftInstructions.md` and `rubrics.md` one last time.
